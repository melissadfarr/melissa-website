I received a BA from UNC-Chapel Hill (Linguistics, 2010) and completed year one of graduate school (2010-2011) at North Carolina State University. My research focused on sociophonetic linguistics and discourse analysis and heavily utilized The North Carolina Language and Life Project database (NCLLP). I left the program in 2011 with the intention of completing it at a later time.

While an undergraduate, I presented a paper at The SouthEastern Conference for Linguistics (SECOL LXXVI). As a graduate student, I presented papers at ASU (Linguistics/TESOL Symposium 17) and NCSU (AEGS 2). In addition to my research, I maintained a 4.0 GPA while in the NCSU linguistics graduate program.

Two of my responsiblities as a funded research assistant in the program were to assist in the maintainence of the NCLLP database and schedule weekly 'brown bag' seminars. I have included more about my professional experience at NCSU here.
